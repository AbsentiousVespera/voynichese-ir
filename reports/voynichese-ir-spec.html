Word-internal order is monotone: q at 0.00, benches at ~0.2, gallows ~0.3, nucleus ~0.5, then l · r · y · m · n stacked against the right edge. Words are walks through a positional template — the signature of a regular language.

The order-1 regular model is MDL-optimal; longer context does not pay for itself. The word language is Regular (star-free, slot-ordered), LL(1)-trivial, PEG-compatible. Above the word: class-bigram mutual information 0.110 bits vs 0.014 shuffled — real but an order of magnitude weaker than word-internal structure. Strongest supra-word rule: P(next word begins q | current ends y) = 0.281 vs 0.07–0.11 otherwise. No evidence of constituency.

11Parser specification

Deterministic finite automaton over 30 glyph units; 267 edges (count ≥ 15) recognize 96.9% of tokens. FIRST: o .225 · ch .159 · q .143 · d .093 · sh .084. LAST: y .404 · n .159 · l .155 · r .153.

Generative precision 75.3%: three of four random walks through the automaton are attested Voynich words. The recognizer is simultaneously a near-exhaustive generator — the lexicon ≈ the language.

13Ambiguity report

Unit segmentation: deterministic under longest match (h never initial, so bench ligatures cannot conflict). The DFA is conflict-free. The slot grammar written as a CFG is massively ambiguous (6,465 types admit multiple slot assignments) because slot alphabets overlap — the language is unambiguous; that presentation is not. Canonical form: the DFA, slots as leftmost-longest view.

Engineering verdict
  1. The Word is the statement. Nearly all structure lives inside the token: rigid slot template, ~4 bits/unit of internal constraint against ~0.1 bit of word-order constraint.
  2. The lexicon is an enumeration, not a vocabulary. 75% of random grammar walks are attested words; frequent types form one edit-1 cluster — an opcode space, not a dictionary.
  3. Layout is syntax. BEGIN marker (92% ascender), short terminal lines, line-final descenders, one-word labels — the AST was recoverable without reading a glyph.
  4. Two registers, one language. Currier A and B are disjoint regions of the same word-space under the same grammar.
  5. Language class: Regular. A complete parser is 267 weighted edges.

Constraint for any future semantic theory: it must reproduce (a) regular slot morphology, (b) lexicon≈language density, (c) weak Markov word order with the q|y coupling, (d) layout-level BEGIN/END marking, (e) the A/B split under one grammar. The measured profile is statistically unlike natural-language lexica and unlike simple substitution of one — both preserve lexical sparsity and word-order information that this corpus does not have.
